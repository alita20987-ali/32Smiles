Professional Education in Dental Care

Oral Hygiene Basics

Daily habits like proper brushing, flossing, and diet choices form the foundation of good oral health and help prevent most common problems.

Brush Twice Daily

Use soft-bristled brush and fluoride toothpaste for 2 minutes. Angle at 45° to gums and use gentle circular motions to remove plaque effectively.

Learn Technique
Floss Every Day

Clean between teeth to remove food and plaque brushing misses. Use 18 inches of floss, curve in C-shape around each tooth, and slide gently gumline.

Flossing Guide
Smart Eating Habits

Limit sugary snacks/drinks, drink water after meals, and choose tooth-friendly foods like cheese, nuts, and crunchy veggies to protect enamel.

Diet Tips

Common Problems & Prevention

Early awareness and prevention can stop most dental issues before they become painful or expensive.

Cavities (Tooth Decay)

Caused by plaque acids. Prevent with fluoride, brushing/flossing, and limiting sugar. Early signs: sensitivity or dark spots.

Prevention Steps
Gum Disease

Starts with bleeding gums (gingivitis). Prevent by daily cleaning and regular check-ups. Advanced cases can lead to tooth loss.

Spot the Signs
Bad Breath & Sensitivity

Often from poor hygiene or dry mouth. Brush tongue, stay hydrated, and use mouthwash for fresh breath and comfort.

Daily Fixes

Special Care for Kids & Seniors

Tailored tips for different life stages to build lifelong healthy habits.

For Children

Use rice-grain sized fluoride toothpaste (under 3), pea-size (3+), supervise brushing until age 7–8, and start flossing when teeth touch. Limit sweets and schedule first dental visit by age 1.

Kids Tips
For Seniors

Deal with dry mouth, receding gums, or dentures? Brush gently twice daily, floss or use interdental tools, stay hydrated, and visit dentist regularly to catch issues early.
